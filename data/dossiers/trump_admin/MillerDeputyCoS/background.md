# Stephen Miller - Background Dossier

## Biography

**Full Name:** Stephen Miller
**Born:** August 23, 1985 (age 40)
**Birthplace:** Santa Monica, California
**Current Role:** White House Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy & Homeland Security Advisor
**Status:** "Trump's brain," "the prime minister," most influential White House official outside Trump

## Early Life and Conservative Awakening

### Liberal Family Background
- Born in Santa Monica, California to a **politically liberal Jewish family**
- **Childhood conversion**: Became conservative after reading *Guns, Crime, and Freedom* (1994) by NRA CEO Wayne LaPierre in high school
- This was Miller's political awakening - rejected family's liberalism for conservatism

### High School Activism
- Attended Santa Monica High School
- **Vocal critic** of school's perceived liberalism
- Began appearing on **conservative radio talk shows** while still in high school
- Early pattern: confrontational, media-savvy, ideologically committed

## Duke University (2003-2007): Formation of Worldview

### Academic Career
- Attended **Duke University** (2003-2007)
- Studied **political science**
- Graduated 2007

### Campus Conservative Activism

**Polarizing Opinion Columnist:**
- Wrote **"Miller Time"** column for *The Chronicle* (Duke's student newspaper)
- Column was highly polarizing and controversial
- Used platform to attack campus liberalism and political correctness

**Palestine Solidarity Movement Conference (2004):**
- Duke scheduled Palestine Solidarity Movement conference
- Miller was **outraged** at Duke's decision
- **Founded Duke chapter** of David Horowitz's **Students for Academic Freedom**
- Rallied students to oppose the conference
- First major organizational success

**Leadership Positions:**
- **Executive Director** of Duke Conservative Union
- **President** of Duke chapter of Students for Academic Freedom
- Built reputation as aggressive conservative activist

### Intellectual Influences at Duke
- **David Horowitz** - Anti-left activist, founded Students for Academic Freedom
- Conservative media ecosystem
- Campus free speech battles
- Anti-political correctness movement

**Key Formation**: Duke years cemented Miller's identity as culture warrior fighting campus leftism. This became template for later career - identify liberal target, organize opposition, use media, never compromise.

## Jeff Sessions Mentorship (2009-2016): Immigration Restrictionism

### Joining Sessions' Office
- After graduation (2007), worked briefly before joining Senator Jeff Sessions (R-AL) in **2009**
- Served as **Communications Director** for Sessions
- Sessions was Senate's leading immigration restrictionist
- **This was Miller's political education in immigration policy**

### Sessions' Influence
Sessions introduced Miller to:
- **Anti-immigration think tanks**: Federation for American Immigration Reform (FAIR), NumbersUSA, Center for Immigration Studies
- **Heritage Foundation** (conservative policy organization)
- **Immigration restrictionist intellectual network**
- **Legislative tactics** for blocking immigration bills

### Major Legislative Battle: Gang of Eight (2013)

**The Bill:**
- Border Security, Economic Opportunity, and Immigration Modernization Act of 2013
- Bipartisan "Gang of Eight" compromise (McCain, Schumer, Rubio, Graham, etc.)
- Path to citizenship for undocumented immigrants
- Passed Senate 68-32

**Miller and Sessions' Opposition:**
- **Miller was Sessions' chief strategist** for killing the bill in House
- Crafted messaging: "amnesty," threat to American workers, border security inadequate
- Coordinated with conservative media (Rush Limbaugh, Laura Ingraham, etc.)
- Mobilized grassroots opposition
- **SUCCESS**: Bill died in House of Representatives

**Significance**: This was Miller's breakthrough. He learned he could kill major bipartisan legislation through messaging, media, and grassroots mobilization. Sessions-Miller partnership defeated Republican establishment (Rubio, McCain, Graham) and Democratic leadership (Schumer, Obama).

**Irony for 2025**: Marco Rubio was Gang of Eight member pushing amnesty. Now Rubio is SecState and Miller's close collaborator - both have moved to Trump's hardline position.

## First Trump Administration (2017-2021): The Muslim Ban and Family Separation

### White House Positions
- **Senior Advisor to the President** (2017-2021)
- **White House Director of Speechwriting** (2017-2021)
- Youngest person to hold Senior Advisor role in modern history

### The Muslim Ban (Executive Order 13769, January 2017)

**Policy:**
- Banned entry from seven Muslim-majority countries: Iran, Iraq, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, Syria, Yemen
- Suspended refugee admissions
- Indefinitely banned Syrian refugees

**Miller's Role:**
- **"Key architect"** of the Muslim ban
- Drafted executive order
- Coordinated rollout
- Defended policy in media

**Controversy:**
- Immediate legal challenges (ACLU, state attorneys general)
- Protests at airports nationwide
- Courts blocked enforcement
- International condemnation
- Revised multiple times due to court rulings

**Significance**: Miller's first major policy implementation. Set pattern: aggressive executive action, legal challenges, media firestorm, partial success despite opposition.

### Family Separation Policy (2017-2018)

**Policy:**
- "Zero tolerance" prosecution of border crossers
- **Children separated from parents** as deterrent
- Up to 2,800 children separated
- Some children lost in system, never reunified

**Miller's Role:**
- **"Vocal supporter"** and **"architect"** of family separation
- Argued separation would **deter** asylum seekers
- **Deliberately punitive** measure, not just enforcement
- Defended policy as necessary deterrent

**Quote attributed to Miller**: "If you don't want to be separated from your children, don't cross the border illegally."

**Controversy:**
- Widespread condemnation from both parties
- Resignation of DHS Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen (partially over this)
- Court orders to reunify families
- UN human rights officials called it torture
- Became defining image of Trump administration's immigration cruelty

**End of Policy:**
- Trump signed executive order ending separations (June 2018) due to political backlash
- Miller opposed ending the policy
- Many families never reunified; ongoing litigation

**Significance**: Miller's willingness to use extreme measures (separating children) to achieve immigration goals. No policy was too harsh if it served restrictionist ends.

### Internal White House Battles (2017-2019)

Miller fought continuous battles with administration moderates:

**Opponents:**
- **John Kelly** (Chief of Staff) - wanted order and restraint
- **Gary Cohn** (Economic Advisor) - supported immigration for economic growth
- **H.R. McMaster** (National Security Advisor) - wanted NATO commitment language
- **James Mattis** (Defense Secretary) - opposed extreme policies
- **Rex Tillerson** (Secretary of State) - opposed Muslim ban rollout
- **Kirstjen Nielsen** (DHS Secretary) - resisted some immigration measures

**Miller's Tactics:**
- **Outlasted opponents** - they all left, Miller stayed
- **Direct access to Trump** - others had to go through Kelly, Miller could bypass
- **Ideological purity** - never wavered, never compromised
- **Media combat** - attacked opponents publicly
- **Policy sabotage** - drafted executive orders circumventing moderates

**Result**: By 2019-2020, most moderates were gone. Miller had won. Second term shows result - no moderates to fight.

### NATO Article 5 Controversy (2017)

- Miller and Steve Bannon **removed language** from Trump's Brussels NATO summit speech
- Removed commitment to **Article 5** (collective defense)
- National Security Advisor H.R. McMaster fought to restore language
- **Dispute** between McMaster (traditional alliance supporter) and Miller (nationalist skeptic)
- Language eventually restored but incident showed Miller's alliance skepticism

### Leaked Emails Controversy (2019)

- Southern Poverty Law Center (SPLC) obtained **900+ emails** Miller sent to Breitbart News (2015-2016)
- Emails showed Miller sharing **white nationalist content**
- Promoted stories from white nationalist websites
- Shared articles on "white genocide" and immigration's demographic impact
- SPLC labeled Miller an **extremist** based on emails

**Miller's Response:**
- Dismissed as "attack from discredited organization"
- Refused to apologize or explain
- Trump administration defended Miller
- Miller kept his position

**Significance**: Emails revealed Miller's ideological formation and sources. Showed he was influenced by white nationalist framing of immigration, not just economic arguments.
